## Introduction

When building GIS web applications, one of the most important decisions is choosing the right mapping library.

Two of the most popular options are **Leaflet** and **OpenLayers**.

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} and :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} both solve mapping problems, but in very different ways.

---

## Leaflet — Simplicity First

Leaflet is lightweight and easy to use.

### Strengths:
- Simple API
- Fast learning curve
- Great for basic maps
- Huge community

### Best for:
- Small to medium apps
- Basic GIS visualization
- Quick prototypes

---

## OpenLayers — Power & Control

OpenLayers is more advanced and enterprise-ready.

### Strengths:
- Advanced spatial operations
- WMS/WFS support
- Projection handling
- High scalability

### Best for:
- Enterprise GIS systems
- Complex spatial data
- National-level platforms

---

## My Real Experience

In real projects, I used Leaflet for fast development and OpenLayers for complex GIS systems.

Each tool has its place — it's not about which is better, but which is appropriate.

---

## Conclusion

- Use Leaflet for simplicity
- Use OpenLayers for power and scalability

Choosing the right tool early saves months of refactoring later.
